About

Who Am I?

Hi I'm Sruthi Kurada. I am a rising senior at AMSA Charter School who is passionate about the application of computer science to aid the healthcare and biotechnology sector. I am currently a student researcher at the BayesMendel Lab in Harvard Medical School working on developing a novel all-cancer risk assessment model. Additionally I am a student researcher in the MIT PRIMES program, working on identifying molecular pathways for the potential drug, Ergothioneine.

In addition to research, I am an advocate for democratizing access to STEM education opportunities. Towards this goal, I founded MetroHacks EmpowHer, a year-long team competition for high school girls to create a computer science project that benefits their community. My mission is to encourage more female students to pursue a career in engineering and provide young women an inclusive environment to hone their technical and business skills. Over the past year, the EmpowHer community has grown to over 250 participants and 85 mentors!

I look forward to utilizing the entrepreneurial skillset that I developed while leading my own research projects and founding EmpowHer to serve my community through education and technology.
